# Star Wars Unlimited: Official Rules & Formats

## 1. Premier Format (Standard Constructed)
This is the primary competitive format for Star Wars Unlimited.

### Deck Construction Rules
- **Deck Size:** Minimum **50 cards** in the draw deck.
- **Leader & Base:** Must include exactly **1 Leader** and **1 Base**.
- **Card Copies:** Up to **3 copies** of any single card (by name).
- **Sideboard:** Up to **10 cards** (optional).

### Suspended / Ban List (As of Nov 5, 2025)
*Note: FFG uses a "Suspended" list rather than a permanent "Ban" list, implying cards may return to legality in the future.*

- **Boba Fett** (*Collecting the Bounty*) - Suspended
- **DJ** (*Blatant Thief*) - Suspended
- **Jango Fett** (*Concealing the Conspiracy*) - Suspended
- **Triple Dark Raid** - Suspended
- **Force Throw** - Suspended (Added Nov 2025)

## 2. Twin Suns Format (Multiplayer)
A casual, multiplayer format designed for 3-4 players (free-for-all).

### Deck Construction Rules
- **Leaders:** **2 Leaders** per player.
  - **Restriction:** Both leaders must share at least one aspect (e.g., both have the Villainy aspect).
- **Deck Size:** Minimum **50 cards**.
- **Card Copies:** **Singleton Format** (Highlander).
  - You may only have **1 copy** of any given card in your deck.
  - Leaders and Base are exempt from this limit (1 Base, 2 Leaders).

### Gameplay Notes
- Players share a "blast" counter or similar mechanic to track game progress/elimination.
- The goal is to defeat opponents' bases.
- Winning conditions can vary by tournament organizer (e.g., last player standing vs. most HP remaining when first player is eliminated).

---

## 3. Trilogy Format
A restricted format often used for specific organized play seasons or community leagues.

### Deck Construction Rules
- **Rules:** Follows standard **Premier** construction rules (50+ cards, 3 copies max).
- **Card Pool Restriction:** Decks may ONLY contain cards from the first three sets:
  1. *Spark of Rebellion*
  2. *Shadows of the Galaxy*
  3. *Twilight of the Republic*

### Validation
- Community tools like **SWUDB** offer a "Trilogy Validator" to ensure deck legality for this format.
